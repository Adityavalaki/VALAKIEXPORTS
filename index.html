OUR HERITAGE

Rooted in Agriculture, Committed to Global Export

Valaki Exports Co. is a prominent member of the Valaki Brothers group of companies. Our family has been passionately involved in large-scale farming in the fertile region of Mahuva, Gujarat—India's primary cultivation hub for high-pungency onions and garlic—for over a century.

Leveraging this deep agricultural inheritance, we manage everything from seeds, crop nutrition, and harvesting, to manufacturing and worldwide export logistics. Our sister processing plant, Euro Foods Industries (est. 2013), operates state-of-the-art dehydration and packaging lines. While Euro Foods services domestic supplies, Valaki Exports Co. handles direct global export operations, catering strictly to the quality standards of international food manufacturers and distributors.

FROM FARMS TO PORT

Our End-to-End Processing Pipeline

Our quality assurance process is governed by our core belief: "Quality is never an accident". Here is how we guarantee export-quality products.

01
🌱

Raw Material Selection

We source raw onions, garlic, and spices directly from contract farming lands in Mahuva and major agricultural hubs, ensuring Good Agricultural Practices (GAP).

02
🧹

Washing & Sorting

Materials undergo rigorous mechanical washing, cleaning, peeling, and manual inspection to isolate and remove any defective crop or foreign matter.

03
✂️

Precision Dehydration

Vegetables are sliced or minced and dried using automated belt-dryers at controlled low temperatures, keeping essential oils, taste, and nutrition intact.

04
🔬

Milling & Sorting

Dried products are milled into granules or powders, then passed through high-intensity magnetic separators, metal detectors, and optical color sorters.

05
📦

Hygienic Packaging

Finished goods are packed in food-grade poly-lined corrugated boxes, multi-layer paper bags, or bulk bags to prevent moisture entry and preserve freshness.

06
🚢

Logistics & Customs

Containers are loaded directly at our factory warehouse, sealed under customs supervision, and dispatched to major ports (e.g. Mundra, Pipavav) for shipping.
